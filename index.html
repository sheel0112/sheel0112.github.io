About me

Why I got into product, and why I'm leaning hard into fintech.

Why product?

I moved into product to bridge the space between engineering detail and the real-world pressure of trading desks. I enjoy making complicated systems usable, reliable, and fast for the people who depend on them.

Sports trading risk taught me to deliver under time pressure, measure exposure in real time, and communicate decisions with clarity. Those habits transfer directly to regulated fintech environments.

Fintech and banking excite me because the stakes are higher: more users, tighter controls, and opportunities to build trust through great tooling. I'm looking for teams that care about safety and velocity in equal measure.

How I work

  • Curious: always pairing with engineers and analysts to understand the edge cases.
  • Global outlook: experienced across UK, EU, and North America with a bias for inclusive teams.

Decision-making style

How I balance speed, safety, and clarity when product decisions get complex.

  • Data-informed, but willing to move on intuition when the signals are directional.
  • Calm in pressure-driven environments with clear escalation paths.
  • User-first and regulation-aware — I optimise for trust, not just throughput.
  • Collaborative across functions with crisp, concise communication.

How I think about risk

  1. Identify early: map operational, data, and regulatory risks with teams.
  2. Quantify: impact/likelihood scoring and clear owners for each risk.
  3. Communicate: simple status rituals and visual cues everyone understands.
  4. Balance: design guardrails that protect users while enabling product bets.

Future goals: Join a fintech or banking team where I can lead risk-aware product delivery, modernise internal tooling, and mentor teams on decision quality.
