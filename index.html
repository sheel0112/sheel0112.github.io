About me

Why I got into product, and why I'm leaning hard into fintech.

Why product?

I moved into product to bridge engineering detail with the real-world pressure of trading desks. I enjoy making complicated systems usable, reliable, and fast for the people who depend on them.

Sports trading risk taught me to deliver under time pressure, measure exposure in real time, and communicate decisions clearly. Those habits transfer directly to regulated fintech environments.

Fintech and banking excite me because the stakes are higher: more users, tighter controls, and opportunities to build trust through great tooling.

How I work

  • Collaborative: open communication with engineering, data, design, and ops.
  • Calm under pressure: comfortable shipping when risk is high and time is short.
  • Curious: always pairing with engineers and analysts to understand edge cases.
  • Global outlook: experienced across the UK, EU, and North America.

Decision-making style

How I balance speed, safety, and clarity when decisions get complex.

  • Data-informed, but willing to move on directional signals.
  • Clear escalation paths under pressure.
  • User-first, regulation-aware, trust-focused.
  • Collaborative across engineering, data, design, ops.

How I think about risk

  1. Identify early: map operational, data, and regulatory risks.
  2. Quantify: impact/likelihood scoring + clear owners.
  3. Communicate: simple rituals and visual cues.
  4. Balance: guardrails that enable product bets safely.

Why fintech appeals to me

Personal motivation: I enjoy building trust in systems people rely on daily — whether that's checking exposure or checking a balance.

Shared skills: Sports trading taught me probabilistic thinking, real-time decisioning, and balancing speed with safety.

Future goals: I want to lead risk-aware product delivery in a fintech or banking environment.
